<h3>🇮🇹 Scalea – The Heart of the Cedar Riviera</h3>

<p>When people think of Calabria, they often imagine rugged mountains, wild nature, and turquoise seas.
And <strong>Scalea</strong> is where all of that comes together – a town that lives both its history and its present.</p>

<p>Since ancient times, Scalea has been inhabited thanks to its strategic location on the coast of the Tyrrhenian Sea.
The old part of town – <em>Scalea Vecchia</em> – stretches along the hillside, offering postcard-like views.
Narrow cobblestone alleys, weathered palaces, old churches, and the shadows of the past will lead you to the ruins of a Norman castle.
Every stone whispers a story.</p>

<p>In contrast, the lower and modern part – <em>Scalea Nuova</em> – lives in the present.
Cafés smelling of espresso, markets with fresh fruits, evening promenades filled with locals and tourists, gelato shops, and a beach
that stretches for several kilometers along the coast.
A morning swim, an evening pizza with a sunset view – what more could you ask for?</p>

<p>The city's most iconic landmark is the <strong>Torre Talao</strong>, an old watchtower that is now one of the most photographed places in all of Calabria.
And if you're interested in history on a deeper level, be sure to visit the local archaeological museum,
housed in a former noble palace.</p>

<p><strong>Scalea</strong> is an ideal starting point for trips along the <strong>Cedar Riviera</strong>, named after the rare citrus fruit – the cedar –
which is typical for this area.
Nearby, you'll also find the <strong>Pollino National Park</strong>, one of the largest natural areas in Italy,
perfect for hiking and wilderness lovers.</p>

<p>🌅 Whether you're seeking peace, adventure, gastronomy, or history,
<em>Scalea</em> will surprise you with its authenticity and warmth. It’s not just a resort – it’s a place to live.</p>
